EZ-Fold Chair

The EZ-Fold project developed a space-efficient, ergonomic chair to assist elderly individuals in putting on shoes, addressing limited space and stability needs in Korean apartments. The design features foldable elements, sturdy materials, and mechanisms to aid in sitting and standing safely.

Design & Fabrication Process

Research & Design Opportunity

The project began by identifying a critical design opportunity: the daily challenge faced by elderly individuals in Korea when putting on shoes, particularly due to physical limitations and the confined spaces of typical Korean apartment doorways. Extensive research was conducted, including:

  • Analysis of existing solutions and their shortcomings in terms of space efficiency and ergonomic support.
  • Comprehensive market research, highlighting the increasing elderly population in Korea and the growing need for solutions promoting independence.
  • Development of user personae and in-depth client interviews to understand specific pain points related to balance, leg strength, hip movement, and the difficulty of reaching shoes.
  • Analysis of population and anthropometric data to inform optimal dimensions and safety requirements for the chair.

Exploration of Ideas

The initial phase of idea generation involved brainstorming without immediate constraints, allowing for a wide range of creative solutions. This open exploration aimed to capture diverse concepts for the chair's folding mechanisms, support structures, and overall form. Various sketches and preliminary concepts were documented to explore different approaches to the problem.

Development of Ideas

Several promising ideas from the brainstorming phase were selected for further development. This involved creating detailed 3D CAD models and physical scaled models to thoroughly evaluate their feasibility, ergonomics, and space-saving potential. Finite Element Analysis (FEA) was conducted on various critical points of each model to understand stress distribution and ensure structural integrity. A total of four distinct design concepts were rigorously developed, with their respective advantages and disadvantages carefully considered to inform the final design decisions.

Orthographic Assembly Drawing

The final design of the EZ-Fold Chair is a culmination of iterative development and rigorous analysis, incorporating the most successful elements from previous concepts. Key components and materials were meticulously selected for durability, safety, and user comfort.

Key Components:

  • Seat Cushioning: Faux leather and polyurethane foam for comfort and durability.
  • Seat Body & Chair Base: CNC milled 40mm Maple Wood Board, chosen for its strength, durability, and ease of cleaning.
  • Linear Bearing: Ensures smooth up/down movement of the chair.
  • Stainless Steel Pole & Peg End: Provides high tensile strength and stability.
  • Back Bracket: Laser-cut stainless steel for secure wall mounting.
  • Gas Strut: Integrated to assist with opening/closing and to absorb impact when sitting, enhancing user safety and convenience.
  • Joining Components: Screws, nuts & bolts, and stainless steel U-brackets for strong, disassemblable connections.

Manufacturing Plan

A detailed manufacturing plan was developed to guide the fabrication of the EZ-Fold Chair prototype. This plan outlines a step-by-step process, specifying the equipment, estimated scheduling, quality control measures, and risk assessments for each stage. Key steps include:

  • Sawing and sanding of maple wood components (seat body, chair base, peg ends).
  • Attaching the leather seat cushion and linear bearings.
  • Precision laser cutting of metal sheets for the back bracket.
  • Assembly of the seat body and chair base with stainless steel poles and peg ends.
  • Attachment and calibration of the gas strut.
  • Final installation and secure mounting of the chair onto a wall.

Throughout the manufacturing process, strict quality control checks were integrated to ensure precise measurements, secure connections, and overall product stability, prioritizing user safety and functionality.
