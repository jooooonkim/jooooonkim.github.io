Automated Robotic Arm

This project involved the comprehensive design, simulation, and analysis of a 6-axis robotic arm, primarily focusing on pick-and-place operations in an industrial setting. The goal was to develop a robust and efficient robotic system capable of precise movements and payload handling.

Initial CAD model of the robotic arm in SolidWorks.

Kinematic simulation of the arm's workspace in MATLAB.

Key Features & Technologies:

  • Design & Modeling: Utilized SolidWorks for 3D modeling and assembly, ensuring all components were accurately dimensioned and manufacturable.
  • Kinematics & Dynamics: Developed forward and inverse kinematic models in MATLAB to control arm movements and understand its operational envelope. Dynamic analysis was performed to assess motor requirements and joint loads.
  • Control System: Implemented a basic PID control loop for joint position control, simulated in Simulink.
  • FEA Analysis: Conducted Finite Element Analysis on critical components (e.g., arm links, base plate) using ANSYS to ensure structural integrity under various loading conditions.
  • Material Selection: Researched and selected appropriate materials (e.g., aluminum alloys for lightness and strength) to optimize performance and cost.

Challenges & Solutions:

One of the primary challenges was achieving precise control over the arm's end-effector position, especially when handling varying payloads. This was addressed by refining the kinematic model and tuning the PID controller parameters through iterative simulations. Another challenge was minimizing vibrations during rapid movements, which was mitigated by optimizing the arm's geometry and considering damping properties of materials.

Learnings:

This project significantly enhanced my understanding of robotic system design, control theory, and the practical application of CAD/CAE tools. It also provided valuable experience in troubleshooting complex engineering systems and optimizing designs for specific performance criteria.

Tools Used:

SolidWorks MATLAB Simulink ANSYS Kinematics Control Systems
